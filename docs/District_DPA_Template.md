## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties have entered into or intend to enter into a separate Service Agreement (the "Service Agreement") pursuant to which Vendor will provide the District access to Waypoint, a discipline management and DAEP compliance platform;

WHEREAS, in connection with providing those services, Vendor may receive, access, or process "Education Records" as defined under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act ("FERPA"), 20 U.S.C. § 1232g, and its implementing regulations at 34 C.F.R. Part 99, and "Covered Information" as defined under the Texas Student Privacy Protection Act, Texas Education Code §§ 32.151-32.158;

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to establish the terms under which Vendor will handle such Education Records and Covered Information;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises contained herein and in the Service Agreement, the Parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Education Records"** means records, files, documents, and other materials that (i) contain information directly related to a student and (ii) are maintained by the District or by Vendor on behalf of the District, as defined under FERPA, 20 U.S.C. § 1232g(a)(4).

**1.2 "Student Data"** means any personally identifiable information derived from Education Records, including but not limited to: student names, student ID numbers, dates of birth, grade levels, demographic information, disciplinary records, special education and Section 504 status, behavioral assessments, transition plans, and incident records.

**1.3 "Covered Information"** means information in any media or format that is not publicly available and is any of the following about a student: (a) information in the student's education record; (b) the student's name, home address, telephone number, email address, or other information that allows physical or online contact; (c) discipline records; (d) test results; (e) special education data; (f) juvenile delinquency records; (g) grades; (h) behavioral assessments; (i) disabilities; or (j) any other information defined as "covered information" under Texas Education Code § 32.151, as may be amended. Where there is overlap, "Covered Information" and "Student Data" shall be construed to provide the broadest protection to student privacy.

**1.4 "Authorized Purpose"** means providing the Waypoint platform services described in the Service Agreement, including incident tracking, SPED/504 compliance workflow, transition plan management, behavioral tracking, data import, and reporting services for the District's authorized staff, consistent with a "school purpose" as defined under Texas Education Code § 32.151.

**1.5 "Authorized Users"** means District employees and contractors designated by the District who are granted access to the Waypoint platform by the District administrator.

**1.6 "Subprocessor"** means any third party engaged by Vendor to process Student Data or Covered Information on Vendor's behalf in connection with providing the services.

**1.7 "Security Incident"** means unauthorized acquisition, access, use, modification, or disclosure of Student Data or Covered Information that compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of such data, consistent with the definition of "breach of system security" under Texas Business and Commerce Code § 521.002.

## ARTICLE 2 — DESIGNATION AS SCHOOL OFFICIAL AND OPERATOR

**2.1 School Official Status.** For purposes of FERPA, the District hereby designates Vendor as a "school official" with a "legitimate educational interest" in Education Records, as those terms are used in 34 C.F.R. § 99.31(a)(1)(i)(B). This designation applies solely to the extent necessary for Vendor to perform its obligations under the Service Agreement. Vendor shall be under the direct control of the District with respect to the use and maintenance of Education Records, consistent with 34 C.F.R. § 99.31(a)(1)(i)(B)(1).

**2.2 Scope of Legitimate Educational Interest.** Vendor's legitimate educational interest is limited to accessing and processing Student Data solely to provide the Waypoint platform services to the District, as described in the Authorized Purpose.

**2.3 Compliance with FERPA.** Vendor agrees to comply with FERPA requirements applicable to school officials, including the requirement that Vendor use Education Records only for the Authorized Purpose and not disclose Education Records without the District's written consent except as permitted by FERPA. The District is responsible for including Vendor in its annual FERPA notification to parents as a school official with legitimate educational interest, to the extent required by 34 C.F.R. § 99.7.

**2.4 Operator Status Under Texas Law.** Vendor acknowledges that it constitutes an "operator" as defined under Texas Education Code § 32.151, and agrees to comply with all obligations imposed on operators under Texas Education Code §§ 32.151-32.158, as may be amended.

## ARTICLE 3 — PERMITTED AND PROHIBITED USES

**3.1 Permitted Uses.** Vendor may collect, use, and process Student Data only for the Authorized Purpose and as necessary to:
- (a) Provide and maintain the Waypoint platform services to the District;
- (b) Respond to technical support requests from the District;
- (c) Ensure the security and integrity of the platform;
- (d) Comply with applicable law or legal process directed at Vendor.

**3.2 Prohibited Uses.** Vendor shall NOT:
- (a) Sell, rent, lease, or trade Student Data or Covered Information to any third party;
- (b) Use Student Data or Covered Information for advertising, marketing, or targeted advertising of any kind;
- (c) Build a profile of a student for any purpose other than the Authorized Purpose;
- (d) Share Student Data with other school districts or organizations without the District's express written consent;
- (e) Use Student Data to develop or improve any product or service for use by or sale to third parties other than in connection with providing services to the District;
- (f) Use Student Data for any commercial purpose unrelated to providing services to the District;
- (g) Disclose Student Data to any party other than Authorized Subprocessors without prior written consent of the District, except as required by law.

**3.3 Aggregated/De-Identified Data.** Vendor may use de-identified, aggregated data that cannot reasonably be used to identify any individual student for product improvement, statistical analysis, or benchmarking purposes, provided that Vendor applies de-identification methods consistent with 34 C.F.R. § 99.3 and takes reasonable technical measures to prevent re-identification.

**3.4 Compliance with Texas Student Privacy Protection Act.** In addition to the prohibitions in Section 3.2, Vendor shall comply with all obligations imposed on operators under Texas Education Code §§ 32.151-32.158, including without limitation the prohibitions on targeted advertising (§ 32.152(a)(1)), non-school-purpose profile building (§ 32.152(a)(2)), and sale of Covered Information (§ 32.152(a)(3)).

**3.5 Response to Legal Process.** If Vendor receives a subpoena, court order, or other legal process requiring disclosure of Student Data, Vendor shall: (a) notify the District as soon as practicable and before disclosure, to the extent permitted by law; (b) cooperate with the District in seeking a protective order or other relief; and (c) disclose only the minimum Student Data required to comply with the legal process.

## ARTICLE 4 — DATA SECURITY

**4.1 Security Program.** Vendor shall maintain a comprehensive written information security program containing appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to:
- (a) Protect against anticipated threats or hazards to the security or integrity of Student Data;
- (b) Protect against unauthorized access or use of Student Data;
- (c) Ensure the proper disposal of Student Data.

**4.2 Minimum Security Standards.** Vendor's security program shall include, at minimum:
- (a) Encryption of Student Data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent standard;
- (b) Encryption of Student Data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher;
- (c) Role-based access controls limiting access to Student Data to Authorized Users and necessary Vendor personnel;
- (d) Multi-tenant data isolation ensuring District data is logically separated from other customers' data at the database layer;
- (e) Audit logging of access to Student Data with user identification and timestamp;
- (f) Annual or more frequent security assessments or third-party audits of the security program.

**4.3 Security Documentation.** Upon written request, Vendor will make available to the District its most recent SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent security audit documentation, subject to a non-disclosure agreement.

**4.4 Personnel.** Vendor shall ensure that all personnel with access to Student Data are trained on data privacy and security requirements no less frequently than annually and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 6 — DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

**6.1 Vendor Notification to District.** In the event that Vendor discovers or reasonably suspects a Security Incident, Vendor shall:
- (a) Notify the District within **seventy-two (72) hours** of discovering the Security Incident, or sooner if required by applicable law;
- (b) Provide a preliminary written incident report within **five (5) business days** of discovery, describing the nature of the incident, the data involved, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address the incident;
- (c) Cooperate fully with the District's investigation of the Security Incident;
- (d) Assist the District in complying with any notification obligations the District may have under applicable law, including the Texas Identity Theft Enforcement and Protection Act (Texas B&C Code § 521.053); and
- (e) If the Security Incident affects **250 or more Texas residents**, notify the Texas Attorney General as soon as practicable and **not later than thirty (30) days** after determining the breach occurred, via the electronic form on the AG's website, as required by Texas B&C Code § 521.053(i), as amended by SB 768 (eff. Sept. 1, 2023).

**6.2 Notification Content.** The initial notification shall include, to the extent then known: (a) a description of the Security Incident; (b) the categories and approximate number of students affected; (c) the likely consequences; and (d) the measures Vendor has taken or proposes to take to address the incident and mitigate its effects.

**6.3 Individual Notification.** Individual notification to affected students or parents, where required by Texas B&C Code § 521.053, must be made as quickly as possible and not later than **sixty (60) days** after the date on which the breach is determined to have occurred. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to determine notification responsibilities. Vendor shall bear its own costs for notifications arising from its breach of this Agreement.

**6.4 No Vendor Notification of Third Parties Without Consent.** Except as required by applicable law (including Section 6.1(e)), Vendor shall not notify students, parents, or other third parties of a Security Incident without the District's prior written consent.

## ARTICLE 7 — DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

**7.1 Parent/Guardian Rights.** Vendor acknowledges that parents and eligible students have rights under FERPA to inspect, review, and request amendment of Education Records. Vendor shall:
- (a) Refer any requests from parents or eligible students regarding Education Records to the District within five (5) business days of receipt;
- (b) Provide reasonable assistance to the District in responding to such requests within any applicable statutory timeframe;
- (c) Not independently fulfill or deny such requests without District direction.

**7.2 District Access.** The District shall have the right to access, correct, update, or delete Student Data at any time through the Waypoint platform administrator tools or by written request to Vendor.

---

## ARTICLE 8 — DATA RETENTION AND RETURN

**8.1 Retention During Term.** Vendor will retain Student Data for the duration of the Service Agreement and for ninety (90) days following termination or expiration of the Service Agreement ("Retention Period"), to allow the District time to retrieve its data.

**8.2 Data Export.** Upon request at any time during the term, or within the Retention Period following termination, Vendor shall provide the District with a complete export of all Student Data within thirty (30) days of the request, including: (a) tabular records in CSV and/or JSON format; and (b) uploaded document files (incident attachments, compliance documents) in a ZIP archive in their original file formats.

**8.3 Deletion.** When the District requests deletion of Student Data or Covered Information, or at the conclusion of the Retention Period, Vendor shall:
- (a) Delete all Covered Information from production systems **not later than sixty (60) days** after the date of the District's request, consistent with Texas Education Code § 32.154;
- (b) Delete Student Data from backup systems as backup files expire in the ordinary course, which shall be no later than thirty (30) days after production deletion (total maximum: ninety (90) days from request);
- (c) Provide written certification of deletion to the District within thirty (30) days of completing production deletion.

**8.4 Legal Hold.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, Vendor may retain Student Data for a longer period if required by applicable law or legal process, in which case Vendor shall notify the District to the extent permitted by law and shall continue to apply all security obligations in this Agreement to the retained data.

---

## ARTICLE 9 — DATA LOCATION

**9.1 United States Processing.** Vendor shall process and store all Student Data exclusively within the United States. Vendor shall not transfer Student Data to any location outside the United States without the District's prior written consent.

---

## ARTICLE 10 — AUDITS AND ASSESSMENTS

**10.1 Audit Rights.** Upon at least thirty (30) days prior written notice, and no more than once per calendar year (except following a Security Incident), the District may conduct or commission a security audit of Vendor's systems and practices relating to Student Data. Costs of such audit shall be borne by the District, except that following a confirmed Security Incident caused by Vendor's breach of this Agreement, audit costs shall be borne by Vendor.

**10.2 Audit Substitution.** In lieu of a direct audit, Vendor may satisfy audit rights by providing the District with its most recent SOC 2 Type II report, third-party penetration test summary, or equivalent security assessment, subject to a non-disclosure agreement. The District may accept or reject this substitute at its discretion.

---

## ARTICLE 11 — TERM AND TERMINATION

**11.1 Term.** This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and shall continue for the duration of the Service Agreement, and for the Retention Period following termination or expiration of the Service Agreement.

**11.2 Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement, provided that the breaching Party fails to cure such breach within said thirty (30) day notice period, in which case this Agreement shall terminate automatically at the expiration of such period.

**11.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement, Vendor shall comply with the data return and deletion obligations set forth in Article 8.

**11.4 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon sixty (60) days prior written notice to the other Party. Termination for convenience does not relieve Vendor of its data return and deletion obligations under Article 8, and does not entitle either Party to a refund of pre-paid fees unless specified in the Service Agreement.

## ARTICLE 12 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**12.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to conflict of law principles. Any disputes arising under this Agreement shall be resolved in the state or federal courts located in [County — insert Clear Path's operating county], Texas.

**12.2 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Service Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, and negotiations.

**12.3 Amendment.** This Agreement may not be amended except by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

**12.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**12.5 No Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any right or provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such right or provision.

**12.6 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures are acceptable and have the same legal effect as original signatures.

**12.7 Relationship of Parties.** Vendor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, or partner of the District. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a joint venture or partnership between the Parties.

**12.8 Conflict.** In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the Service Agreement with respect to data privacy and security obligations, the terms of this Agreement shall control.

**12.9 Notices.** All formal notices required under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by: (a) certified mail, return receipt requested; (b) nationally recognized overnight courier; or (c) email with written confirmation of receipt, to the addresses in the signature block below. Either Party may update its notice address by written notice to the other Party. Operational communications (support requests, export requests) may be conducted by email without the formalities of this section.

**12.10 Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that Vendor may assign this Agreement without consent in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of Vendor's assets, provided that: (a) the acquirer assumes all obligations of Vendor under this Agreement in writing; (b) Vendor provides written notice to the District within thirty (30) days of the assignment; and (c) the District retains the right to terminate this Agreement within thirty (30) days of receiving such notice if the acquirer is a direct competitor of the District or presents a material data privacy risk, in Vendor's reasonable determination.

**12.11 Force Majeure.** Neither Party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance resulting from causes beyond that Party's reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, epidemic or pandemic, war, terrorism, government orders, or failures of third-party infrastructure providers. The affected Party shall notify the other Party promptly and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resume performance as soon as practicable. Force majeure does not excuse payment obligations.
