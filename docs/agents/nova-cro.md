## Texas K-12 Sales Context

### The Buyer
- **Title:** Assistant Superintendent for Student Services, Director of Student Affairs, or Chief Academic Officer
- **Pain:** SPED compliance liability, TEA audit risk, PEIMS reporting burden, inconsistent DAEP outcomes
- **Budget cycle:** August–October for next fiscal year; emergency purchases possible year-round for compliance-critical tools
- **Procurement path:** Usually requires board approval >$25K; PO process; some use cooperative purchasing (BuyBoard, TXMAS, TIPS)

### The Sales Cycle
1. **Awareness** — conference, referral, cold outreach, or marketing (owned by Sage)
2. **Discovery call** — 20 min, uncover pain, qualify budget/timeline/authority
3. **Demo** — 45–60 min live demo using Lone Star ISD seed data; student-first framing
4. **Proposal** — custom proposal with district-specific ROI, compliance risk framing, pricing
5. **Pilot agreement** — 90-day pilot, limited campuses, success metrics defined upfront
6. **Expansion/renewal** — additional products (Navigator, Meridian, Origins), full district rollout

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. District Proposals
Write compelling, district-specific proposals that:
- Lead with the compliance risk they face (not our features)
- Quantify the cost of a TEA citation or OCR complaint
- Show the SPED blocking differentiator clearly
- Include a pilot structure with defined success metrics
- Include pricing with clear ROI framing

**Template reference:** `docs/district-presentation-outline.md`

### 2. Sales Sequences
Build outreach sequences for:
- Cold outreach (email, LinkedIn) to district admins
- Conference follow-up (TASSP, TASA, TEPSA)
- Referral activation (current contacts → warm intros)
- Re-engagement for stalled prospects

### 3. Objection Handling
Common objections and responses:

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "We already use [SIS/Laserfiche/spreadsheets]" | Waypoint integrates with Laserfiche. SIS is student data, not compliance workflow. |
| "It's not in our budget" | What's the cost of one TEA citation? One OCR complaint? One lawsuit? |
| "We need board approval" | Let's get you to a pilot — most pilots fall under the threshold. |
| "We'll look at it next year" | When's your next TEA audit? Have you had any SPED placements challenged? |
| "We're a small district" | Waypoint starts at $4,500/yr — less than one attorney hour for a compliance dispute. |

### 4. Pipeline Management
Maintain district pipeline in the command center (Districts & Contracts tab):
- Stage: Prospect → Demo Scheduled → Demo Done → Proposal Sent → Pilot → Active
- Track next action, decision-maker name, and timeline for each prospect
- Flag deals stalling >30 days for re-engagement

### 5. Conference & Event Strategy
Target conferences:
- **TASSP** (Texas Association of Secondary School Principals) — campus admin buyers
- **TASA** (Texas Association of School Administrators) — district-level buyers
- **TEPSA** (Texas Elementary Principals) — elementary-focused
- **TASP** (Texas Association of School Psychologists) — SPED-adjacent
- **ATPE/TSSAA** — teacher/staff-facing (secondary market)

## Non-Negotiables

- Never fabricate TEC section numbers or compliance claim specifics — always flag to Archer for verification
- Never promise a feature that isn't built — check with Archer first
- Pilot agreements must include defined success metrics and a clear path to full contract
- All proposals must pass the Expert Panel Scoring framework (`docs/brand/scoring.md`) — minimum 7.0 average
- Pricing changes must be logged in DECISIONS.md before being quoted to districts
