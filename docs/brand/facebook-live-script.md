## SEGMENT 1 (1:30 - 5:00) — The Compliance Problem Nobody Talks About

*Screen: District Compliance Overview dashboard*

"This is your district compliance overview. Every campus, color-coded. Red means someone is in trouble right now.

See this? **Overdue MDRs.** That number is clickable — takes you right to the student. No digging through files. Waypoint caught it automatically because when a SPED student gets referred to DAEP, the system **blocks the placement** until the manifestation determination is complete. You physically cannot skip it.

That one feature alone would have saved districts I know from TEA corrective action.

Now look — **students approaching the 10-day removal limit.** Every DAEP referral that comes through Waypoint automatically counts toward cumulative removal days. At 7 days, amber warning. At 10, it's red — and for SPED students, that's the federal trigger for a manifestation determination.

If your campuses are also using Navigator to track ISS and OSS, those days roll into the same count. That's how you get the complete picture — Navigator catches the early removals at the campus level, Waypoint catches the DAEP referrals, and the compliance dashboard shows you the total across both."

**Key selling point:** This isn't a report you run once a month. It's live. Right now. Every campus."

---

## SEGMENT 2 (5:00 - 9:00) — The DAEP Referral Flow

*Screen: New Incident page*

"Let's walk through what happens when a campus refers a student to DAEP.

The principal creates an incident. Student, offense code — these are actual Texas Education Code offenses, not made-up categories. Consequence type: DAEP. Assigned days. Upload the transcript and current schedule — Waypoint requires these documents before the referral can move forward.

Now here's where it gets powerful. The referral enters an **approval chain.** Campus behavior coordinator, counselor, SPED coordinator if the student is SPED, all the way up to your director of student affairs. Each person sees only what's waiting for them. Each person adds their notes. There's a complete paper trail.

*Click through to show approval chain tracker*

If someone denies it or sends it back for corrections, the campus gets a notification and the reason is right here. No phone tag. No 'I sent that email two weeks ago.'

And if the student is SPED or 504? **Compliance hold.** The placement is blocked. Period. The checklist has to be completed — MDR, parent notification, IEP review — before the student can be placed. This is automatic. Nobody has to remember to check."

---

## SEGMENT 3 (9:00 - 12:00) — Seat Management & Campus Visibility

*Screen: DAEP Dashboard → Home Campus Allocations widget*

"Now I want to show you something districts have been asking for forever.

Every campus in your district has an allocation of DAEP seats. High School gets 10. Middle School gets 8. Elementary gets 4. You set this in Settings — takes 30 seconds.

Now look at this widget. **Per campus: allocated, active, pending, available.** Your high school principal logs in and sees — 'I have 3 of my 10 seats being used, 2 more pending approval, 5 available.' They know exactly where they stand without calling you.

And this — **no-shows.** A student completed orientation but never showed up to DAEP. That seat is still held. An admin can release it with one click. Before Waypoint, that seat just... disappeared into the void.

*Switch to hs-principal view*

This is what a campus principal sees. Not the whole DAEP operation — just **their students.** Their seat allocation. Their kids returning soon. Their pending approvals. Clean. Focused. No noise."

---

## SEGMENT 4 (12:00 - 16:00) — The Return: Where Every District Drops the Ball

*Screen: Transition Plan Detail page with handoff banner*

"This is where I get passionate, because this is where students fall through the cracks.

A student finishes their DAEP days. In most districts, somebody sends an email to the home campus and hopes for the best. The student walks back in and nobody has a plan.

In Waypoint, when DAEP marks the incident complete, the transition plan **automatically flips** to 'Pending Home Campus.' The home campus counselor opens it and sees this amber banner: 'Accept Handoff & Adjust Plan.'

They click Accept. A modal pops up: 'What needs to change now that this student is back? What worked at DAEP? What should we continue? What should we drop?'

*Show the post-return adjustments field*

They write their notes. Click Accept. Done. The plan is now **owned by the home campus** with a clear record of who accepted it, when, and what adjustments were made.

Then the 30-day, 60-day, and 90-day reviews kick in. Each one shows the reviewer exactly what supports are in place for **this specific student** — the behavioral plan, the academic plan, the interventions, the post-return notes. No guessing.

**This is what keeps students from coming back to DAEP.** And it's what protects you if a parent ever says 'nobody told us what was happening.'"

---

## SEGMENT 5 (16:00 - 19:00) — Navigator: Catching Them Before DAEP

*Screen: Navigator Dashboard*

"One more thing I want to show you. This is Navigator — it's for your home campuses managing ISS and OSS.

See this orange panel? **DAEP Risk — Proactive Intervention Needed.** Waypoint is watching your ISS/OSS data. When a student racks up multiple placements and failed supports, the risk score goes up. 70+ is high. 85+ is critical.

This is the early warning. Before the DAEP referral. Before the compliance headache. Your campus counselor sees this and says, 'We need to intervene now.'

And when a student does come back from DAEP — Navigator picks them right back up. Shows what supports they had. One click to import those supports. The circle closes.

**Navigator is how you stop filling DAEP seats. Waypoint is how you manage the ones that are full.**"

---

## SEGMENT 6 (19:00 - 22:00) — The Settings Districts Actually Need

*Screen: Settings page*

"Real quick — let me show you what you control.

**DAEP Seat Allocations** — set per campus, self-serve.
**Discipline Matrix** — your district's rules. First offense fighting: ISS. Second offense: OSS. Third: DAEP. Fully configurable by offense, occurrence, and grade band. This is YOUR matrix, not a one-size-fits-all.
**Custom Interventions** — add your own. Peer mediation. Restorative circles. Whatever your district uses. They show up alongside the system-wide catalog.
**Offense Codes** — TEC Chapter 37 codes are built in. Add your own district codes on top.
**Notification Preferences** — per user. Stop the email flood.

Everything is per-district. Your settings don't affect anyone else's. Multi-tenant from the ground up."
