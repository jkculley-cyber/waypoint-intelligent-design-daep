## Demo Tips for Kim

1. **Don't mention Waypoint or DAEP** unless they ask. If they do: "If your campus ever needs DAEP management, we have that too — but Navigator is the product that solves your daily problem."
2. **Let them click around.** After the walkthrough, hand them the keyboard. They'll find things that matter to them.
3. **Ask what they're using now.** Usually it's Excel, a shared Google Sheet, or nothing. Any of those = strong pitch.
4. **Emphasize the effectiveness data.** APs get asked to justify their decisions constantly. This is their evidence.
5. **The DPA is the close.** Getting it signed means they're committed. Don't leave without discussing it.
