## The Problem Waypoint Solves

Every school year, thousands of Texas students are placed in Disciplinary Alternative Education Programs — better known as DAEP. For most districts, managing that process means juggling paper checklists, spreadsheets, email chains, and manual PEIMS data entry. It means a principal approving a placement and hoping someone remembered to check whether the student has an IEP. It means a behavior coordinator tracking 30-day review deadlines on a sticky note. It means a campus receiving a student without knowing the placement was ever approved.

When something falls through the cracks, districts don't get a warning — they get an audit finding. Or worse, an OCR complaint. Under IDEA and Texas Education Code Chapter 37, failing to complete a manifestation determination for a SPED student before DAEP placement isn't a paperwork oversight — it's a federal violation. The consequences are real: corrective action plans, loss of federal funding, and in extreme cases, litigation.

Waypoint exists because Texas districts deserve better than spreadsheets for one of their highest-stakes compliance responsibilities.

## What Waypoint Is

Waypoint is a cloud-based discipline management and DAEP compliance platform built specifically for Texas K-12 school districts. It is the only platform that physically blocks DAEP and expulsion placements for students with IEPs and 504 plans until every required compliance step is complete.

Waypoint is not a Student Information System — it works alongside SIS platforms like Skyward, PowerSchool, and Infinite Campus. Waypoint is the compliance and workflow layer that those systems don't provide. Districts import their student rosters into Waypoint and use it to manage the full discipline lifecycle from the moment an incident occurs through the student's return to their home campus.

Waypoint is a product of Clear Path Education Group, LLC, a Texas-based education technology company. It is built from the ground up on Texas Education Code Chapter 37, with Texas SPED procedures, PEIMS action codes, ARD committee workflows, and native integration with Laserfiche — the most widely used document management system in Texas school districts.

Waypoint serves the full spectrum of staff involved in student discipline and DAEP placement.

**District Administrators and Assistant Superintendents** use Waypoint to monitor compliance across all campuses, manage the approval chain for DAEP placements, and pull PEIMS-ready discipline data for state reporting.

**Principals and Assistant Principals** use Waypoint to create incident reports, submit placement recommendations, receive approval notifications, and track students currently in DAEP placement from their campuses.

**SPED Coordinators** use Waypoint to manage manifestation determination records, ARD meeting documentation, Behavior Intervention Plan reviews, and FAPE documentation — all linked directly to the incident that triggered them.

**Counselors and Campus Behavior Coordinators** use Waypoint to monitor repeat offender alerts, manage transition plans, conduct 30/60/90-day structured reviews, and track student progress after a placement.

**DAEP Staff** use Waypoint's orientation kiosk and daily behavior tracking kiosk to record student arrivals, track daily behavior scores, and manage placement schedules.

**Parents and Guardians** have read-only access through a dedicated parent portal that shows them their child's incident record, the placement decision, the transition plan, and a countdown of days remaining in the placement. Parents can digitally acknowledge receipt of placement notices directly in the portal.

**Teachers** can submit referrals through a simplified referral form — no full incident access required — which feeds directly into the campus incident workflow.

Waypoint supports twelve distinct role types with granular permissions. Non-administrator staff are scoped to their assigned campuses — they can only see students from their own campus. District-wide administrators see all campuses. This campus scoping is enforced at the database level, not just the application layer.

## The Full Discipline Lifecycle in Waypoint

### Step 1: Incident Creation

When a discipline incident occurs, an authorized staff member creates an incident record in Waypoint. The incident form captures the student involved, the date and location, the offense category (mapped to Texas PEIMS action codes), the consequence type — whether ISS, OSS, DAEP, or expulsion — and the number of placement days.

If the offense involves fighting or assault and multiple students need to be kept apart, staff can add Separation Orders directly to the incident record. Separation Orders identify which specific students must be kept away from the placed student at the DAEP campus. This data is protected under FERPA and is visible only to district staff — it never appears in the parent portal.

For districts using Laserfiche for their DAEP approval workflows, Waypoint integrates directly. A daily Excel report from Laserfiche is imported into Waypoint, which automatically creates or updates student records and incident records, using the Laserfiche Instance ID as a deduplication key. No manual double-entry.

### Step 2: The Approval Chain

Once an incident is created, it enters the approval workflow. Waypoint supports a configurable approval chain — typically moving from the campus principal to a district-level administrator. Each approver receives an email notification when action is required.

Approvers can review the full incident record, add notes, approve, or deny. When a placement is approved, the incident status changes to "Approved" and the campus is notified. When denied, the reporter receives an email explaining the denial.

This approval chain creates a documented, timestamped audit trail for every placement decision — something paper-based processes fundamentally cannot provide.

### Step 3: SPED and 504 Compliance Blocking

This is Waypoint's most critical differentiator. When a student with an active IEP or 504 plan is placed in DAEP or recommended for expulsion, Waypoint's compliance engine fires automatically.

A compliance checklist is generated and attached to the incident. The checklist includes every required step: manifestation determination, ARD committee meeting documentation, Behavior Intervention Plan review, FAPE documentation, and parent notification. The checklist is linked to the incident permanently.

Here is what makes Waypoint unique: the placement cannot proceed until the compliance checklist is complete. This is not a reminder. This is not a warning message. The system physically prevents advancing the placement for a SPED or 504 student if mandatory compliance steps are outstanding. Under IDEA, this protection is not optional — it is the law. Waypoint makes it structurally impossible to skip.

### Step 4: Placement Scheduling and Orientation

Once a placement is approved — and for SPED students, once the Change of Placement ARD is complete — the campus can schedule the student's DAEP orientation.

If the district has configured orientation time slots — specific days of the week and times when DAEP staff are available — Waypoint presents a slot picker showing available appointment times up to 90 days ahead, with real-time capacity tracking. Staff select a slot and it is booked. If no orientation configuration exists, staff enter a date and time manually, with the system enforcing that only future dates can be selected.

If a student misses their scheduled orientation, their status automatically updates to "Missed" and the campus receives a notification. The campus can then immediately reschedule through a dedicated rescheduling flow.

### Step 5: DAEP Orientation Kiosk

On the day of orientation, the student checks in at the DAEP campus using Waypoint's orientation kiosk — a tablet-based interface that requires no staff involvement. The student enters their student ID number and, optionally, a PIN or date of birth for a second verification factor.

When the student checks in, the orientation kiosk completes a structured intake process: the student reviews behavioral expectations, reviews program rules, and fills out a reflection form that becomes part of their permanent record. The orientation kiosk completion automatically updates the student's placement status.

### Step 6: Daily Behavior Kiosk

Each day the student is at DAEP, they check in at the daily behavior kiosk — a separate tablet station from the orientation kiosk. The daily kiosk logs attendance, captures a daily behavior score, and confirms the student is physically present at the DAEP campus.

A student's first daily kiosk check-in after orientation is the official confirmation that their placement has started. It automatically updates the placement status from "Orientation Complete — Awaiting Start" to "Active."

DAEP staff can view daily behavior logs for all enrolled students. The behavior data feeds into the student's transition plan reviews.

### Step 7: Transition Plans and Structured Reviews

Every student in DAEP placement has a Transition Plan in Waypoint. The transition plan documents the goals, supports, and interventions planned for the student's time at DAEP and their return to the home campus.

What distinguishes Waypoint's transition plans from a simple text document is the structured 30/60/90-day review system. At each review interval, staff complete a structured review form that captures specific metrics: the number of office referrals in the period, the student's CICO (Check-In Check-Out) score, academic grades, attendance rate, and a behavioral score. These metrics are tracked over time and visualized as charts, giving staff a clear picture of whether the student is progressing.

Transition plan reviews are linked to the originating incident, creating a complete, unbroken record from the original discipline event through the student's return.

### Step 8: Return to Campus

When a placement ends, the incident is resolved in Waypoint and the student's record is updated. The full history — incident, approval chain, compliance checklist, orientation record, daily behavior logs, and transition plan reviews — remains permanently attached to the student's record.

If the student re-offends, Waypoint's repeat offender alert system detects the pattern and automatically generates an alert for district administrators.

## Repeat Offender Alert System

Waypoint continuously monitors student discipline patterns and generates automatic alerts when dangerous thresholds are crossed. There are four alert triggers:

**Red Flag — 2nd DAEP Placement:** When a student receives their second DAEP placement within the same school year, a red alert is generated. This is a critical escalation signal — a student cycling through DAEP twice in one year is at serious risk and likely needs a fundamentally different intervention.

**Red Flag — 3 or More ISS in 30 Days:** When a student receives three or more in-school suspension placements within a 30-day window, a red alert fires. This pattern often predicts DAEP placement and signals that ISS alone is not an effective intervention.

**Yellow Flag — Same Offense Three or More Times:** When a student is disciplined for the same offense category three or more times, a yellow alert fires. This indicates a specific behavioral pattern that targeted intervention might address.

**Yellow Flag — 5 or More Referrals in 30 Days:** When a student accumulates five or more discipline referrals within 30 days, a yellow alert fires. High referral frequency across offense types often indicates an underlying need the school has not yet identified.

Alerts appear in the dedicated Alerts page, in the sidebar notification badge, and on the DAEP dashboard. Staff can move an alert through a workflow: New → Acknowledged → In Progress → Resolved. Each alert can have notes attached documenting what interventions were attempted.

The Alerts page also displays the Separation Orders hot box — a FERPA-protected panel showing all students who have active keep-away orders, the students they must be kept apart from, and a link to the originating incident.

## DAEP Dashboard

The DAEP Dashboard is the command center for DAEP staff and district administrators. It provides a real-time operational view of everything happening across the district's DAEP program.

The dashboard includes:

**Active Enrollments Table:** A live list of every student currently in DAEP placement, showing their name, home campus, placement start date, planned return date, days remaining, transition plan status, and compliance flags. Students with SPED designations are flagged with a purple SPED badge. Students with 504 plans are flagged with a blue 504 badge. English Language Learners are flagged. Students with active separation orders carry an orange SEP badge.

**Approval Flow Panel:** Shows all incidents currently awaiting approval at each step of the chain — pending, under review, and recently approved.

**Orientation Schedule Panel:** Shows upcoming orientation appointments and missed orientations grouped by home campus.

**Pending Placement Start Panel:** Shows students who have completed orientation but have not yet signed in on the daily behavior kiosk — meaning their placement is technically scheduled but hasn't physically started.

**Missed Orientations Panel:** Shows students who missed their scheduled orientation and need to be rescheduled.

## Reports and PEIMS Export

Waypoint's Reports module provides district administrators with the data they need for compliance reporting, board presentations, and state submission.

Reports include incident volume over time, breakdown by offense type, breakdown by campus, DAEP placement duration analysis, and student recidivism rates. All reports are visualized with charts and can be filtered by date range, campus, and offense category.

Every report can be exported to PDF or Excel with one click. Bulk incident export allows administrators to select multiple incidents and export them together.

For Texas state reporting, Waypoint generates a PEIMS-formatted discipline data export as a CSV file, pre-coded with the correct PEIMS action codes for each incident type. This dramatically reduces the manual work of preparing state submissions.

## Parent Portal

Waypoint's parent portal gives families transparent, real-time access to their child's discipline record without requiring them to call the school or wait for a letter.

When a placement is approved, parents can log in and see the incident record, the placement decision, the number of days assigned, the days remaining, and the transition plan. A countdown timer shows exactly how many days remain in the placement.

Parents can digitally acknowledge that they have received and reviewed the placement notice — creating a documented record of parental notification that is permanently attached to the incident.

The parent portal is strictly read-only and shows only each parent's own children. Sensitive data — including separation orders, internal compliance notes, and staff-only flags — is never visible in the parent portal.

## Discipline Matrix

Waypoint includes a configurable Discipline Matrix that maps offense codes to recommended consequences and required support interventions. The matrix is a district-level configuration that applies across all campuses.

The matrix lists every offense type the district recognizes, the consequence range for each offense (warning, detention, ISS, OSS, DAEP, or expulsion), and the required support interventions — such as parent conference, behavior intervention, counseling referral, or restorative practice — that must accompany each consequence level.

When a staff member creates an incident, the discipline matrix auto-suggests the appropriate consequence based on the offense, ensuring consistency across campuses and reducing individual variation in disciplinary decisions.

## Teacher Referral Flow

Teachers can submit a discipline referral directly from a simplified referral form — no access to the full incident management system required. The referral captures the student, the behavior observed, the date, and any supporting narrative.

Referrals submitted by teachers queue in the campus administrator's dashboard for review. The administrator can convert a referral into a full incident, add consequence information, and begin the approval process — or dismiss the referral with a note. Teachers receive a notification when their referral is acted upon.

## Security and Compliance

Waypoint is built with FERPA compliance as a core design principle, not an afterthought.

Every table in the database has a district identifier column. Row-Level Security policies in PostgreSQL enforce that users can only access data belonging to their own district — this isolation is enforced at the database engine level, not just the application layer. A compromised application session cannot reach another district's data.

Campus scoping for non-administrator staff is enforced the same way — at the database level.

All data is encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher. Passwords are stored as bcrypt hashes — Waypoint staff have no ability to retrieve a user's password. Supabase Auth includes HaveIBeenPwned breach detection, blocking passwords that have appeared in known data breaches.

The kiosk interfaces — used by students who are not authenticated users — access data exclusively through Supabase Database Functions with security invoker controls. Even the kiosk cannot run a direct SQL query against the student table.

Waypoint's infrastructure runs on Amazon Web Services (AWS us-east-1 — Northern Virginia) via Supabase, a SOC 2 Type II certified managed database platform. The frontend application is delivered via Cloudflare Pages with DDoS protection. All student data remains within the United States.

A full FERPA Compliance Statement, Subprocessor List, and links to all third-party Data Processing Agreements are published at clearpathedgroup.com/security.html.
