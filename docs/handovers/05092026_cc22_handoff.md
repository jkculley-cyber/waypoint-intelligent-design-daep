**2026-05-09 — Architecture & Technology:**

> **`audit_log` retention follows TASB Local Records Retention Schedule for Texas K-12 — 7 years on student-discipline-related audit rows. Implementation: monthly partitioning when the table crosses ~500K rows; never DELETE discipline rows in <7 years.** Marlene R2 finding (CC22): the new fail-loud audit triggers (migration 073) write `to_jsonb(OLD)+to_jsonb(NEW)` blobs on every UPDATE — at her 9,500-student district's volume (~3,400 incidents/yr × ~6 lifecycle UPDATEs each + compliance + transition_plan churn), tens of MB/year, growing forever. **Retention rule:** all audit_log rows where `entity_type IN ('incidents','compliance_checklists','transition_plans','transition_plan_reviews','compliance_override_requests')` have a 7-year minimum retention from `created_at`. After 7 years they may be archived to cold storage but not deleted. **Partitioning strategy (deferred until row count crosses ~500K):** monthly RANGE partitions on `created_at`. Migration drafted as scaffold (not yet applied). **TASB reference:** Local Schedule SD item 5.2.001.

## Lessons worth keeping

- **Multi-voice cadence stops at engineering ceiling, not at round number.** Three voices flat (Reyes / Chen / Patricia at their tier) + practitioner score barely moving = R4 not warranted on engineering grounds. Same pattern that stopped Beacon at R3 (Marsha 84% "ship it") and Navigator at R3 (Marsha 84%). The cadence is state-driven, not schedule-driven.
- **Engineering ≠ procurement.** Marlene's 12% → 16% over 2 patch rounds is the slowest practitioner trajectory of any product. Her R2 thesis ("engineering improvements don't compress procurement timelines") was empirically validated by R3. The remaining work to convert her is escrow + legal opinion + reference district + SIS — none of which is a migration.
- **Convergent finding pattern.** The unanimous R2 "frontend not built" finding came from 4 voices independently — that's the highest-confidence audit signal. The single half-week of frontend work that closed it produced the largest tier promotion of the cadence (Patricia MIXED-defensible → TRULY PROTECTIVE).
- **Patricia's framing is a reusable test.** "What I'd tell my client" is the bar that converts compliance-engineering credit into customer-protection credit. The rule the audit produced: "build exactly what she said to build, no more, no less" — and the verdict moved one tier per round.
- **Representational liability against unfixed architectural gaps.** Reyes-named: a parent-facing UI that asserts immutability the architecture doesn't actually guarantee creates a Doe v. Beaumont ISD-style adverse-inference attack. P1's 1-line copy edit defuses without shipping the architectural fix. Reusable for any product where the UI claims something the underlying system can't yet enforce.
- **CLI deploy > dashboard deploy when JWT migration is in flight.** Multiple Supabase projects are mid-migration from HS256 (legacy) to ES256/JWKS (new) JWT signing. Dashboard-deployed functions sometimes inherit stale verifier config; CLI deploy with explicit `--no-verify-jwt` flag is more deterministic. Reusable for next Waypoint Edge Function deploy.
- **Citation hygiene catches itself.** TWO citation errors caught + fixed in the same session via targeted grep + cross-reference (§37.0061 → §37.023 verified against an existing marketing-site reference; Beacon R4 verdict labeled wrong, verified against handover transcripts). DECISIONS 2026-04-26 TEC citation policy is doing what it was written for.
