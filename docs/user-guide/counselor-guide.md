## 1. Your Role in the DAEP Workflow

As counselor or CBC, you're the long-term advocate for students in DAEP. While principals and admins handle the discipline decision, you handle the support and re-entry plan:

| When | Your Action |
|------|------------|
| Student is placed in DAEP | Create the initial transition plan |
| Days 25–35 | Conduct the 30-day review |
| Days 55–65 | Conduct the 60-day review |
| Days 85–95 | Conduct the 90-day/discharge review |
| Red alert fires | Review student history, develop intervention response |
| Student returns from DAEP | Coordinate re-entry with campus team |

## 2. Transition Plans — Creating and Managing

### Where to Find It
**Transition Plans** in the main menu

### Creating an Initial Transition Plan
As soon as a student's DAEP placement is activated:
1. Go to **Transition Plans → + New Plan**
2. Select the student (must have an active DAEP incident)
3. Select plan type: **Initial**
4. Set start date = consequence start date
5. Set end date = projected consequence end date

### Plan Fields

**Academic Goals:**
What academic progress is expected during the placement?
- Targets for credit recovery or grade-level work
- Specific teacher/course accommodations

**Behavioral Goals:**
Observable, measurable behavior targets:
- "Student will follow staff instructions on the first request 90% of the time"
- "Student will remain in designated area without redirection for 60-minute blocks"

**Social-Emotional Support:**
What counseling or therapeutic supports are in place?
- Individual counseling sessions (frequency, provider)
- Group skills sessions (e.g., conflict resolution, emotional regulation)
- Community referrals

**Re-Entry Plan:**
What happens when the student returns?
- Which campus and homeroom?
- Any schedule modifications needed?
- Peer check-in plan?
- Parent communication plan?

### Activating a Plan
Plans start as **Draft**. When complete and shared with the DAEP campus team:
1. Click **Activate Plan**
2. Status changes to **Active**
3. The plan is now visible to all staff with access to this student

## 3. 30/60/90-Day Reviews

Waypoint automatically calculates review due dates from the plan's start date. You do NOT need to set them manually.

| Review | Due Date |
|--------|----------|
| 30-Day | Start date + 30 calendar days |
| 60-Day | Start date + 60 calendar days |
| 90-Day / Discharge | Start date + 90 calendar days |

### Tracking Overdue Reviews
The **Transition Plans** page shows:
- 🔴 Red **"Overdue"** badge on any plan with a missed review
- 🟡 Yellow **"Due Soon"** badge when review is within 7 days
- Red banner at top: **"X transition plan reviews overdue — click a plan to complete the review"**

### Conducting a Review
1. Click the student's plan row
2. Click **Add Review**
3. Complete the review form:
   - Meeting date
   - Who attended (note required attendees)
   - Behavioral progress rating
   - Academic progress rating
   - Goal updates — mark goals as met, in progress, or not met
   - Notes and recommendations
   - Adjusted re-entry date if needed
4. Click **Save Review**
5. The next review date updates automatically

### What to Discuss in Each Review

**30-Day Review:**
- Is the student attending consistently?
- Are they engaging with behavioral goals?
- Any signs of crisis or need for additional support?
- Family communication — are parents engaged?

**60-Day Review:**
- Measurable progress toward behavioral goals
- Academic status — on track for credit recovery?
- Refine re-entry plan: who are the contacts at the home campus?
- Any interventions to add or discontinue?

**90-Day / Discharge Review:**
- Final determination: ready for re-entry?
- Re-entry plan finalized and communicated to home campus team
- Transition plan status changes to **Completed**
- Follow-up schedule after re-entry (Waypoint can track this through standard interventions)

## 4. Student Interventions

Interventions are the support services tied to a student's plan. Track what's working and what isn't.

### Linking Interventions to a Plan
1. Open the student's transition plan
2. Scroll to **Interventions** section
3. Click **Add Intervention**
4. Select the intervention type:
   - Academic tutoring
   - Individual counseling
   - Group social skills
   - Family/community services
   - Restorative practices
   - Mentoring
5. Set start date and provider
6. Assign a completion or review date

### Tracking Outcomes
Each intervention has a status:
- **Active** — Currently being provided
- **Completed** — Finished, with outcome notes
- **Discontinued** — Stopped early (note reason)

Completion rates and intervention types are visible in **Reports → Interventions** — useful for demonstrating your program's effectiveness.

## 5. Repeat Offender Alerts

### Where to Find It
**Alerts** in the main menu

As counselor, red and yellow alerts about repeat offenders are your primary cue to intervene.

### Alert Types You'll See Most Often

| Alert | What It Means | Your Action |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| 2nd DAEP | Student placed in DAEP again | Consider intensive evaluation referral (FBA, behavioral health) |
| 3+ ISS in 30 days | Pattern of ISS use | Review whether ISS is effective; consider alternative |
| Same Offense 3x | Habit-pattern behavior | Targeted skill intervention around that behavior |
| 5+ Referrals | General escalating pattern | Comprehensive student support plan |

### Taking Action on an Alert
1. Click the alert → read the trigger detail
2. Click **Acknowledge** — this logs that you've seen it
3. Click **Mark In Progress** — you're working on it
4. Document what you're doing in the notes field
5. Click **Resolve** when the intervention response is complete

> **Do not dismiss alerts without action.** Dismissal should be reserved for confirmed false positives (e.g., same incident counted twice). Resolved = action was taken.

## 6. Reviewing Student Incident History

### Where to Find It
**Incidents** → search by student name

As counselor, you can view all incidents for students on your campus. This is useful for:
- Understanding the full pattern before a review meeting
- Verifying that prior interventions are documented
- Identifying students who need a comprehensive evaluation referral

### Reading the Incident Timeline
On a student's profile page, the incident history shows:
- Each incident in chronological order
- Consequence type and days
- Whether the student is SPED/504 (flags on each row)
- Cumulative removal days this year (shown in SPED student view)

> **Pattern recognition:** A student with 4+ referrals for different offenses in 60 days is showing a broad pattern. A student with 4 referrals for the SAME offense is showing a specific behavior that needs targeted intervention. These are different problems requiring different responses.
