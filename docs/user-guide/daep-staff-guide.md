## 1. Student Check-In Kiosk

### What It Does
The student kiosk records daily attendance for DAEP students. Each check-in:
- Marks the student present for that day
- Logs a day toward their required placement days
- Updates their behavioral tracking record

### Accessing the Kiosk
The kiosk runs at a specific URL provided by your DAEP coordinator. It's designed for a dedicated tablet or desktop at the DAEP campus entrance.

### Student Check-In Process

**Step 1 — Student enters ID**
The student types their district student ID number on the kiosk screen.

**Step 2 — Identity confirmation**
The student's name and placement information appear. They confirm by clicking **Check In** (or the kiosk may require a PIN if your district has enabled PIN security).

**Step 3 — Phone bag collection (if enabled)**
If your campus uses phone bag tracking, the student enters their assigned phone bag number.

**Step 4 — Confirmation screen**
A confirmation screen shows:
- Days served so far
- Days remaining
- Behavioral goals for the day

The screen auto-clears after 8 seconds for the next student.

### What to Do if a Student Can't Check In
- **Student's ID not found:** The student may not be enrolled in the system yet. Contact the district admin to verify the placement is activated.
- **Check-in screen shows "placement not active":** The student's placement has not been activated by the campus admin. Contact your principal or admin.
- **Check-in shows "placement ended":** The student's placement end date has passed. Contact admin.

> **Important:** Only students with an **Active** DAEP placement in Waypoint can check in at the kiosk. If a student is physically present at the DAEP campus but their placement isn't showing as Active in the system, contact your admin immediately — their time served won't be counted.

## 2. Daily Behavior Tracking

### Where to Find It
**DAEP Dashboard → Daily Behavior** (or through the student's kiosk check-in)

### What Gets Tracked
Each day a student checks in, you can log behavior data for that day:
- **Behavior score** (1–5 scale, or as configured by your district)
- **Goal completions** — which behavioral goals were met today
- **Incidents or concerns** — any notable behavior (positive or negative)
- **Staff notes** — anything to communicate to counselors or home campus

### Logging Daily Behavior
1. Open the DAEP Dashboard → Active Enrollments
2. Click the student's name
3. Navigate to **Daily Behavior** section on their incident
4. Find today's entry (created automatically on check-in)
5. Rate behavior, note goal completion, add notes
6. Click **Save**

### Viewing Behavior Trends
The student's transition plan page shows a **Daily Behavior Visualization** chart — a trend line showing how their behavior score has changed over the placement. This is useful for:
- Counselor review meetings
- Communication with parents
- Evidence of progress (or lack thereof) in 30/60/90-day reviews

## 3. Orientations

Before a student begins DAEP, an orientation must be completed. This is your first formal contact with the student and family at the DAEP campus.

### Where to Manage Orientations
**DAEP Dashboard → Live Operations → Scheduled Orientations**

### Scheduling an Orientation
Orientations are typically scheduled by the admin or AP after a placement is approved. If you need to schedule one:
1. DAEP Dashboard → Orientations tab
2. Click **Schedule Orientation** for the relevant incident
3. Set date, time, and location
4. Note any required attendees (parent/guardian must be invited)

### Conducting the Orientation

**What orientation covers:**
- Introduce the DAEP campus (location, schedule, expectations)
- Review the student's placement (reason, duration, expectations for return)
- Review the transition plan and behavioral goals
- Collect phone and personal items per school policy
- Explain the daily schedule (arrival, dismissal, transitions)
- Explain the check-in process and kiosk usage
- Have student and parent sign orientation acknowledgment

**After orientation is complete:**
1. Return to the student's incident
2. Scroll to Orientation section
3. Click **Mark Orientation Complete**
4. Enter the date, who attended, and any notes
5. The **"Activate Consequence"** button becomes enabled for the admin

### Missed Orientations
If a student doesn't show for their scheduled orientation:
1. Mark the orientation as **Missed** in the system
2. The DAEP Dashboard shows a **red "Missed Orientations" widget** alerting the AP
3. Contact the family to reschedule within 48 hours
4. A yellow alert is created in the Alerts section

## 4. Phone Return Program

If your campus has a phone storage program (students surrender phones on entry):

### Daily Process
**Morning drop-off:**
1. Student checks in at kiosk — if phone tracking is enabled, they enter their bag number
2. Collect the phone in the labeled bag
3. Store bags in the designated secure location

**Afternoon pick-up:**
1. Student confirms their bag number at dismissal
2. Return phone, have student confirm correct device
3. Log any issue (wrong bag, damaged device, etc.)

### Phone Return Dashboard
**DAEP Dashboard → Phone Return**

This view shows all active students with their assigned bag numbers and daily collection status.

## 5. DAEP Dashboard — Operations View

As DAEP campus staff, the Operations tab of the DAEP Dashboard is your daily command center.

### Key Widgets for DAEP Staff

**Active Enrollments Table**
All students currently in your DAEP program:
- Name, grade, days remaining, campus of origin
- Orange **SEP** badge if student has a separation order (must not be near specific other students)
- Click any row for full incident detail

**Missed Orientations** (red widget)
Students who missed their scheduled orientation. These need follow-up within 24–48 hours.

**Awaiting Placement Start** (amber widget)
Students who were approved for DAEP but haven't had their orientation scheduled yet. Follow up with the campus AP.

**Scheduled Orientations**
Upcoming orientation appointments — use this to prepare for each day.

### Separation Orders
If a student row shows an orange **SEP** badge, that student has an active separation order. This means they must be kept physically separated from a specific other student.

When two students with a mutual separation order are both in your DAEP program:
1. Review the separation order details on their incident
2. Arrange scheduling so they are not in the same physical space at the same time
3. Document any accidental contact or proximity incidents
4. Report separation order concerns to your campus AP immediately

> **Separation orders are legally binding.** Failing to maintain separation after a documented order could expose the district to liability.

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Before school | Review Scheduled Orientations for today |
| Morning | Students check in at kiosk; log phone bag numbers |
| Mid-morning | Review Active Enrollments for any new placements overnight |
| Daily | Log behavioral observations for each student |
| Afternoon | Process phone return; note any issues |
| End of day | Check Missed Orientations widget — follow up within 24 hours |
| Weekly | Review Transition Plans for upcoming review due dates |
