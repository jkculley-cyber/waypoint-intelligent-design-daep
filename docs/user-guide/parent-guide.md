# Waypoint Parent Guide

**Who this is for:** Parents and guardians of students enrolled in a district using Waypoint

This guide explains what you can see in the Waypoint Parent Portal, what actions you can take, and what to do if you have questions.

---

## Accessing the Parent Portal

Your district will send you a login link by email. If you haven't received it:
1. Contact your child's school (main office or counselor)
2. Provide your email address — it must match what the district has on file
3. You'll receive a login link or temporary password

**Your login shows only YOUR children's information.** No other students' records are visible to you.

---

## What You Can See

### Incidents
Any formal discipline incidents involving your child are listed here.

For each incident, you can see:
- **Date** of the incident
- **Offense description** — what happened
- **Consequence** — what action was taken (e.g., ISS 3 days, DAEP 10 days)
- **Status** — where the incident is in the process
- **Required documents** — letters or notices attached

### What You CANNOT See
To protect your child's privacy and the rights of other students:
- You cannot see other students' names or information, even if they were involved in the same incident
- Technical compliance processing details (e.g., internal ARD checklist items) are not shown in the parent view
- Staff notes and internal communications are not visible

### Transition Plans
If your child is in DAEP, their transition and re-entry plan is visible in the portal. You can see:
- Goals that have been set
- Progress notes from reviews
- The re-entry plan timeline

## Acknowledging an Incident

When a new incident is created, you will see an **"Acknowledge Receipt"** button on the incident. This means:
- **You have seen the incident**
- **You confirm receipt of the notification**

This is a formal record. To acknowledge:
1. Click **Acknowledge Receipt**
2. A confirmation box appears showing the incident summary
3. Enter your **full name** — this serves as your electronic signature confirming you received this notice
4. Click **Confirm Acknowledgment**
5. The incident shows a green "Acknowledged" status with the date and time

> **Your acknowledgment is a legal record.** It documents that you received notice of the discipline action. Your signature is stored alongside the timestamp.

**You are acknowledging receipt — not agreeing that the action was appropriate.** If you have concerns about the discipline decision, contact the school directly.

## Your Rights as a Parent

Under **FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act):**
- You have the right to inspect and review your child's education records
- You have the right to request amendment of records you believe are inaccurate
- You have the right to consent to disclosure of records (with some exceptions)

Under **IDEA (if your child has a disability):**
- You have the right to participate in all ARD (IEP) meetings
- You have the right to receive prior written notice before any change in your child's educational placement
- You have the right to dispute a manifestation determination decision
- You must be invited to the Manifestation Determination Review meeting

Under **Section 504:**
- You have the right to participate in 504 placement decisions
- You have the right to appeal 504 decisions through your district's procedures

## When to Contact the School

**Contact the school if:**
- You received no login credentials and your child has an active discipline case
- You believe an incident description is inaccurate
- You were not notified of an ARD/MDR meeting involving your child
- You want to request a copy of the compliance checklist for a SPED/504 DAEP placement
- You have questions about the re-entry plan or transition plan goals

**Your contacts:**
- **Counselor or CBC** — transition plan, re-entry, student support
- **AP or Principal** — incident review, consequence questions
- **SPED Coordinator** — ARD/IEP/MDR questions (for SPED students)
- **504 Coordinator** — Section 504 questions

---

## Privacy & Security

- Your login is personal — do not share it with anyone, including your child
- The portal is secured with FERPA-compliant access controls
- Your child's data is never sold or shared with third parties
- To report a security concern: `privacy@clearpathedgroup.com`
