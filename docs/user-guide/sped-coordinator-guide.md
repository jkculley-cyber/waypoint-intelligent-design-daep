## 1. Why SPED/504 Students Are Different in DAEP

Federal law provides additional protections for students with disabilities when a school seeks to remove them from their educational setting.

**IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)** — SPED students:
- Cannot be removed from their educational placement without a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR)
- Cannot be placed in a DAEP that fails to provide FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education)
- Must have their IEP services continued even in the DAEP setting
- Cannot be removed for more than 10 cumulative school days in a year without triggering IDEA protections

**Section 504 (Rehabilitation Act)** — 504 students:
- Also require a manifestation determination before DAEP placement
- Must have their 504 accommodations maintained in the DAEP setting
- OCR (Office for Civil Rights) has jurisdiction over 504 violations

**Waypoint enforces these requirements.** When a SPED or 504 student is recommended for DAEP, the placement is immediately blocked until every required step is complete and documented.

## 2. When You'll Be Notified

You'll receive notification (system alert + email if configured) when:
- A SPED student is referred for DAEP placement
- A SPED student's cumulative removal days approach 10

### Finding Your Active Compliance Cases
Go to **Compliance** in the main menu. The page shows all active compliance checklists with their completion status:
- 🔴 **Blocked** — Checklist is incomplete; placement is blocked
- 🟡 **In Progress** — Some items completed, not all
- 🟢 **Cleared** — All items complete; placement can proceed

Click any row to go to the incident and the checklist.

## 3. The SPED Compliance Checklist — Step by Step

Navigate to the incident for the SPED student. Scroll to the **SPED/IDEA Compliance Checklist** section.

You'll see the red compliance banner at the top:
```
⛔ DAEP Placement Blocked — IDEA/SPED Compliance Required
[student name] is identified as SPED. Federal law requires completion of the compliance checklist...
Next action: SPED Coordinator or Campus Admin must complete the checklist below.
[Go to checklist ↓]
```

### The 8 Checklist Items

#### 1. ARD Committee Notified *(Required)*
The full ARD committee must be notified of the pending DAEP recommendation.
- Send written notice to all ARD members (teacher, admin, parent, diagnostician, etc.)
- Log the date of notification
- Click to mark complete — system records the timestamp

> **TEC reference:** TEC §29.005(e) — ARD committee has the right to meet and review any change to a student's educational placement.

#### 2. Manifestation Determination Conducted *(Required — see Section 5)*
The ARD committee must convene a meeting specifically to determine whether the behavior was caused by or substantially related to the student's disability.
- This is a formal meeting with parents/guardians in attendance
- Must occur within 10 school days of the disciplinary decision
- After checking this item, you MUST record the result (IS or NOT a manifestation)

#### 3. Current BIP Reviewed *(Required for SPED)*
The student's current Behavior Intervention Plan must be reviewed for implementation fidelity.
- Was the BIP being followed?
- Were there triggers or antecedents that the BIP should address?
- Document your review findings

#### 4. FBA Conducted Within Last Year *(Required for SPED)*
A Functional Behavior Assessment must be current.
- If FBA is more than 12 months old, consider requesting an updated FBA before placement
- If FBA was never conducted and behavior is related to disability, this should be done first

#### 5. Parents Notified of Procedural Safeguards *(Required)*
Parents must receive written notice of their procedural safeguards under IDEA.
- Provide the state-approved procedural safeguards notice
- This can be emailed or hand-delivered (get a signature)
- Log the delivery date

#### 6. FAPE Plan for DAEP Documented *(Required)*
A specific plan for how the student will receive FAPE at the DAEP campus must be documented.
- Which IEP services will be provided?
- What modifications/accommodations apply?
- Who is the responsible service provider at the DAEP campus?
- This becomes part of the student's IEP addendum

#### 7. IEP Goals Reviewed *(Optional — complete if applicable)*
Review current IEP goals to ensure alignment with the DAEP service plan.
- Will the student continue to work toward the same goals?
- Are any goals impractical in the DAEP setting?

#### 8. Educational Services Arranged at DAEP *(Optional — complete if applicable)*
Confirm that the DAEP campus has the staff and resources to deliver the required services.
- Is there a special education teacher on the DAEP campus?
- Are related services (speech, OT, PT) arranged?

## 4. The Section 504 Compliance Checklist

The 504 checklist uses different terminology but follows the same general structure:

| Item | 504 Language | Required |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Team notification | "Section 504 Team Notified" | Yes |
| Manifestation determination | Same — see Section 5 | Yes |
| Behavioral supports | "Behavioral Supports Reviewed" | No |
| FBA | "FBA Considered (if applicable)" | No |
| Parent notification | "Parents Notified of Procedural Safeguards" | Yes |
| FAPE documentation | "FAPE Plan Documented" via 504 plan | Yes |
| Accommodation review | "Accommodation Plan Updated" | No |
| Services arranged | "Educational Services Arranged" | No |

> **Key difference:** 504 has fewer hard requirements than IDEA. However, OCR takes 504 violations seriously and can investigate based on a single parent complaint. Document thoroughly.

## 5. Manifestation Determination (MDR)

The MDR is the most critical step in the SPED compliance checklist. It determines whether the student's behavior was caused by or substantially related to their disability.

### Legal Standard (IDEA §300.530(e))
The behavior IS a manifestation of the disability if:
- The behavior was **caused by** the student's disability, OR
- The behavior was the **direct result of the district's failure** to implement the IEP

The behavior is NOT a manifestation if:
- The ARD committee determines neither of the above applies

### Conducting the MDR
1. Convene the ARD committee within 10 school days
2. Invite the parents/guardians (required)
3. Review the student's IEP, BIP, evaluation data, and relevant incident information
4. Determine: Is the behavior caused by or substantially related to the disability?
5. Document the decision in your ARD paperwork
6. Return to Waypoint and record the result

### Recording the Result in Waypoint
1. Check **"Manifestation Determination Conducted"** in the checklist
2. An amber prompt appears: "Record determination result above — IS or NOT manifestation must be selected to proceed"
3. Select the result from the dropdown at the top of the checklist card:
   - **"Behavior IS a manifestation"** → red box appears; DAEP blocked
   - **"Behavior is NOT a manifestation"** → green box appears; DAEP may proceed

## 6. When Behavior IS a Manifestation

If the MDR result is "IS a manifestation," DAEP placement **cannot proceed.** Waypoint shows:

```
🔴 Behavior IS a manifestation of the student's disability
DAEP placement cannot proceed. The student must remain at the home campus
with appropriate BIP modifications.
```

**Required Actions:**
1. Conduct an FBA (if not recently done or if behavior is new)
2. Develop or revise the BIP to address the behavior
3. Return the student to their current educational placement
4. If the behavior involved weapons, drugs, or serious bodily injury, IDEA provides for a 45-school-day removal to an Interim Alternative Educational Setting (IAES) regardless of manifestation — see IDEA §300.530(g)

### IDEA Special Circumstances (§300.530(g))
Even when behavior IS a manifestation, the district CAN remove a student to an IAES for up to 45 school days if the incident involved:
- **Weapons** — Carrying or possessing a weapon at school or school event
- **Illegal drugs** — Knowingly possessing or using drugs at school
- **Serious bodily injury** — Inflicting serious bodily injury on another person at school

Waypoint shows these exceptions in a blue box when "IS manifestation" is selected. Document which exception applies in the override reason.

---

## 7. When Behavior is NOT a Manifestation

If the MDR result is "NOT a manifestation," DAEP placement may proceed.

**Required next steps:**
1. Continue completing all remaining required checklist items
2. Document the FAPE plan for the DAEP setting
3. Ensure IEP services are arranged at the DAEP campus
4. Provide parents with prior written notice of the change in placement

Once all required checklist items are complete, the compliance hold is automatically cleared and the incident proceeds to the approval chain.

## 8. The IDEA 10-Day Rule

Waypoint tracks cumulative removal days for every SPED student across all consequence types (ISS, OSS, DAEP, in-school suspension).

| Threshold | What Happens in Waypoint |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| 5 cumulative days | Amber warning appears during incident entry |
| 10 cumulative days | **Hard block** — system prevents submission; MDR required before proceeding |

> **This is federal law, not a Waypoint setting.** The 10-day rule cannot be adjusted or turned off.

If an admin attempts to submit an incident that would put a SPED student at 10+ cumulative days, the system displays:
```
⚠ IDEA §300.536 — Removal Limit Reached
This submission would bring [student name] to [N] cumulative school days of removal this year,
triggering mandatory IDEA protections. A Manifestation Determination Review is required before proceeding.
```

The submit button is disabled until the MDR is complete.

## 9. Completing the Checklist

As you complete each checklist item:
1. Click the checkbox next to the item
2. A green checkmark appears and the item turns green
3. The timestamp is recorded (date and time of your click)
4. The progress bar at the top of the banner updates

When all **required** items are checked AND the manifestation determination result is recorded:
- The compliance hold is automatically lifted
- The incident status updates to the next appropriate step
- The admin/principal can continue with the approval chain

## 10. Overriding a Compliance Block

In rare cases, a compliance block can be overridden by an admin with a documented reason. As SPED Coordinator, you should know:

- **You cannot override a block alone** — this requires admin-level access
- Overrides are permanently logged in the audit trail
- The override reason is visible to TEA in any audit
- The legal exposure for an improper override rests with the district, not Waypoint

If an admin is pressuring you to proceed before the checklist is complete, **document your objection in writing** and contact your district's legal counsel.

## 11. The Compliance Report (for TEA Visits)

If TEA arrives for a monitoring visit or an OCR complaint is filed, you need documentation of your compliance process for every SPED DAEP placement.

### Generating the Compliance Report
1. Go to the student's incident
2. Scroll to the SPED Compliance Checklist section
3. Click **Print / Export → PDF**

The report shows:
- Each checklist item with completion timestamp
- The name of the user who completed each item
- The manifestation determination result
- Any override applied (with reason)
- The FAPE plan text

This is a single-page printable document you can hand to a TEA auditor or include in OCR response documentation.

### Best Practice — Keep Reports for Every Placement
After every SPED DAEP placement is completed:
1. Generate and download the compliance report PDF
2. File it with your ARD documentation
3. Keep for the required records retention period (Texas: 5 years minimum)
