# Waypoint Teacher Guide

**Role:** Teacher
**Access:** Submit referrals, view status of your submitted referrals

As a teacher, Waypoint lets you submit a formal discipline referral that goes directly to your AP or principal for review. You don't have to track down paper forms, fill out duplicates, or wonder if your referral was received.

---

## What Teachers Can Do in Waypoint

- Submit a discipline referral
- Track the status of submitted referrals
- View student discipline history (your campus students only)

---

## Submitting a Discipline Referral

### Where to Find It
**Main menu → Submit Referral** (or the direct URL your school provides)

### Step 1 — Select the Student
1. Type the student's name or student ID in the search box
2. A dropdown appears as you type — click the student you mean
3. The student's name, grade, and ID appear in an orange confirmation box
4. Click **Change** if you selected the wrong student

> **Scope:** You will only see students assigned to your campus.

### Step 2 — Fill in Incident Details
| Field | Required? | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Date | Yes | Today's date is pre-filled. Cannot be a future date. |
| Time | No | Helpful for incidents involving security footage or witnesses |
| Location | No | e.g., Classroom 204, Cafeteria, Hallway B |
| Severity | Auto-set | Auto-fills when you select an offense type. You can adjust. |
| Offense Type | No | Strongly recommended — helps AP categorize the referral quickly |
| Description | Yes | The narrative of what happened |

### Step 3 — Select Offense Type (Searchable)
1. Click in the **Offense Type** search box
2. Type a keyword (e.g., "fight", "phone", "disrupt")
3. Up to 10 matching offense codes appear — click the correct one
4. The offense code fills in, and severity auto-updates to match
5. Click **Change** to select a different offense

> **Tip:** If you're not sure which offense code applies, leave it blank and add detail in the Description. Your AP will assign the correct code.

### Step 4 — Description
Write a clear, factual account of what happened. This is the official record. Include:
- What you observed (not what you assume)
- What was said (exact words if possible)
- What happened before and after
- Who else was present

**Good example:**
> "At 10:15 AM in Room 204, student pushed another student from behind during a transition. The other student hit the desk. I verbally directed the student to stop. Student used profane language and left the room without permission."

**Avoid:**
> "Student was being disrespectful and aggressive as usual."

### Step 5 — Review & Submit
1. Click **Review & Submit**
2. A summary screen shows all the information you entered — review it carefully
3. If anything is wrong, click **Back to Edit**
4. If everything looks right, click **Confirm & Submit**

### After Submission
- Your referral is created and sent to your AP for review
- A toast notification confirms it was submitted
- You're taken to the incident detail page where you can see the current status

### Tracking Your Referrals
Go to **Incidents** in the main menu. Your referrals are listed here. Look at the **Status** column:
- **Draft** — Saved but not submitted
- **Submitted** — Sent to AP, awaiting review
- **Under Review** — AP is actively reviewing
- **Approved** — Consequence approved
- **Denied** — Referral was not upheld
- **Returned** — AP sent it back with a note — check the incident for the comment

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I submitted a referral but I don't see it anywhere.**
A: Check **Incidents** in the main menu. If it's not there, it may have been submitted as a Draft. Go to Incidents, filter by Status = Draft.

**Q: Can I edit a referral after submitting?**
A: Not directly. If the referral was returned by your AP, you'll be able to add information. Otherwise, contact your AP to note any corrections.

**Q: What if I don't know the offense code?**
A: Leave the offense type blank. Describe what happened clearly in the Description. Your AP will assign the correct code.

**Q: The student has a SPED or 504 flag. Does that change anything for me?**
A: No — submit your referral the same way. The system automatically triggers additional compliance steps for your AP and SPED Coordinator. Your job is to document what you observed.

**Q: Why can't I select a future date for the incident?**
A: Incidents must be reported for the day they actually occurred. If you're documenting a past incident, select the correct past date. Future dates aren't allowed.
