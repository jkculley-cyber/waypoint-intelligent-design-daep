# Waypoint DAEP Guide for Campus Staff

**For:** Principals, Assistant Principals, Counselors, and Campus Behavior Coordinators

This guide walks you through the complete DAEP process in Waypoint — from referring a student, through their time at DAEP, to welcoming them back to your campus with a plan in place.

---

## Your Dashboard at a Glance

When you log in, your dashboard is scoped to **your campus only**. You'll see:

- **Open Incidents** — referrals in progress for your students
- **Compliance Holds** — SPED/504 students whose placements are paused until compliance steps are completed
- **DAEP Approved** — students approved for DAEP but not yet placed
- **Active Alerts** — repeat offender and compliance alerts for your campus
- **Active Plans** — transition plans you need to monitor or review
- **My Campus DAEP Seats** — how many of your students are currently at DAEP vs. your allocation (more on this below)
- **Returning from DAEP Soon** — students within 5 days of completing their placement

---

## Step 1: Referring a Student to DAEP

### Who does this?
The Campus Behavior Coordinator, AP, or Principal — whoever handles discipline at your campus.

### How?
1. Go to **Incidents** in the sidebar and click **New Incident** (or use **Quick Report** for a faster form).
2. Fill in the student, offense, date, and narrative.
3. Under **Consequence**, select **DAEP Placement**.
4. Enter the number of **assigned days** (the total instructional days the student must serve).
5. Link the appropriate **offense code** from the Texas Education Code.
6. Click **Submit for Approval**.

### What happens next?
Your referral enters the **approval chain**. Depending on your district's configuration, it flows through some or all of these reviewers:

1. Campus Behavior Coordinator
2. Counselor
3. Special Education Coordinator *(only if the student is SPED)*
4. Section 504 Coordinator *(only if the student has a 504 plan)*
5. Student Support Specialist
6. Director of Student Affairs

Each reviewer can **approve**, **deny**, or **return for corrections** with written notes. You can see the full approval history and every reviewer's notes on the Incident Detail page under the Approval Chain section.

### If the student is SPED or 504
Waypoint automatically places a **compliance hold** on the referral. The placement cannot proceed until:
- A Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) is documented
- The compliance checklist is completed

This protects your district from TEC violations. You'll see holds on your dashboard.

---

## Step 2: Tracking the Approval

While your referral moves through the approval chain, it shows up in the **Pending** count on your DAEP Seats widget. You can click through to see exactly where each student's referral stands.

If a referral is **returned for corrections**, it appears in the red "Referrals Needing Attention" section on your dashboard. Open it, read the reviewer's notes, make corrections, and resubmit.

If a referral is **denied**, you'll see it in the same section with the denial reason.

**Tip:** If you're an approver in the chain (e.g., you're the Principal and the referral is at the Principal step), you'll see it in the "Pending Your Approval" widget. Open it to review, add your notes, and approve or deny.

---

## Step 3: Orientation

Once approved, your student needs to attend an orientation at the DAEP campus before their placement begins.

### What you can do
- Go to **Orientations** in the sidebar to see scheduled orientations for your students.
- You can also schedule an orientation from this page.

### What to watch for
- If your student **misses** their orientation, Waypoint generates an alert automatically.
- If your student **completes** orientation but hasn't started at DAEP, they show in the "Pending Placement Start" section. They still count against your campus allocation until they either begin or the seat is released.

---

## Step 4: While Your Student Is at DAEP

Once the student checks in at the DAEP campus on their first day, the incident status changes to **Active**.

### What you see on your dashboard
Your **My Campus DAEP Seats** widget shows four numbers for your campus:

| Tile | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| **Allocation** | The number of DAEP seats your campus has been assigned by the district |
| **Active** | Your students currently serving their placement at DAEP |
| **Pending** | Your students whose referrals are in the approval process |
| **Available** | Allocation minus Active minus Pending (how many more you can refer) |

If the Available number goes **negative** (shown in red), your campus has more students in DAEP than your allocation allows. Talk to your district admin about adjusting.

### Days served vs. days assigned
Waypoint tracks **actual instructional days the student attended DAEP**, not calendar days. If a student is absent, that day doesn't count toward their assigned total. This means a 30-day placement might take longer than 30 calendar days.

### No-shows
If a student completed orientation but never showed up to DAEP (zero check-ins), they're flagged as a **No-Show** with a red badge on the Active drill-down. Their seat is still held.

To free up the seat, an Admin, Principal, or AP can click **Release Seat** on that student's row. This is a one-time override — it removes the student from your active count.

### Earned incentives
DAEP staff may reduce the student's assigned days based on behavior incentives. When this happens, your "Returning from DAEP Soon" list updates automatically — the student moves closer to completion.

---

## Step 5: Your Student Is Almost Done

When a student is within **5 instructional days** of completing their DAEP assignment, they appear in the green **"Returning from DAEP Soon"** card on your dashboard.

Each row shows:
- Student name and grade
- Days served out of total assigned
- **Days remaining** (counts down: 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, 0)

**This is your signal to start preparing.** Click on any student to go to their transition plan. Before the student walks back through your doors, you should:

- Review the behavioral and academic supports in the plan
- Talk with your counselor about what will be in place on day one
- Coordinate with teachers who will have the student in class
- Check whether any schedule changes are needed

---

## Step 6: Student Completes DAEP — The Handoff

When the student has served all their assigned days, the DAEP staff marks the incident as **Complete**.

### What happens automatically
Waypoint flags the student's transition plan with a **"Returning to Home Campus"** status. The next time you or your counselor opens the plan, you'll see an amber banner at the top:

> **Returning to Home Campus — Accept Handoff**
> This student completed their DAEP placement. The home campus needs to review the plan, make any post-return adjustments, and accept ownership.

### What you need to do
1. Click **"Accept Handoff & Adjust Plan"**
2. A form appears asking you to note any **post-return adjustments** — for example:
   - *"Continue the daily check-in/check-out card. Add lunch buddy support. Remove the math tutorial — student caught up during DAEP."*
   - *"DAEP reports student responded well to structured breaks. Implement 10-minute movement breaks in 3rd and 6th period."*
3. Click **Accept Handoff**.

The banner turns green to confirm. The plan is now officially **your campus's responsibility**.

---

## Step 7: Supporting the Student After Return

Once you accept the handoff, the transition plan enters its most important phase — keeping the student on track and out of trouble.

### Scheduled reviews
The plan has **30-day, 60-day, and 90-day review checkpoints** built in. Your counselor is responsible for completing these. Each review captures:
- Office referrals since return
- Grade average and attendance
- Behavioral progress rating
- What's working and what needs to change

On the plan page, the **Review Schedule** card shows whether each review is Pending, Due Soon, Overdue, or Complete. Overdue reviews trigger a red alert banner — don't let these slip.

### Counselor check-ins
Your counselor should be touching base with the returning student regularly during the first 30 days. Each check-in is logged in the Reentry Hub section of the plan with a status:
- **Positive** — student is adjusting well
- **Neutral** — no concerns but no notable progress
- **Concerning** — regression or new issues emerging

If a student hasn't had a check-in in 5 or more days, they'll be flagged.

### Your post-return adjustments
The notes you entered when accepting the handoff are visible on the plan page. These serve as the roadmap for your counselor and teachers — what worked at DAEP, what to continue, and what to change for the home campus environment.

### If the student gets in trouble again
If a new incident is created for the student within 90 days of their return, Waypoint's **repeat offender alert** fires automatically. This is an early warning — it doesn't mean another DAEP referral, but it does mean the support plan needs attention.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why can't I see the DAEP Dashboard or Phone Return pages?**
Those are tools for the DAEP facility staff who manage daily operations at the DAEP campus. As a home campus administrator, your view is focused on your students and their plans. You have everything you need on your dashboard and the Incident/Plan detail pages.

**Q: A student was assigned 30 days but it's been 6 weeks. Why aren't they done?**
DAEP tracks *instructional days the student actually attended*. Absences, holidays, and school closures don't count. A 30-day placement means 30 days of check-ins at the DAEP campus.

**Q: Can DAEP reduce a student's assigned days?**
Yes. DAEP staff can adjust the assigned days based on earned incentives (good behavior, academic progress). When this happens, the days-remaining count on your dashboard updates automatically.

**Q: What if I disagree with a denial in the approval chain?**
Open the incident to see the denial reason and reviewer's notes. You can discuss with the reviewer directly or escalate through your Director of Student Affairs.

**Q: Do I have to accept the handoff, or is it automatic?**
You have to accept it. The amber banner stays on the plan until someone on your campus clicks Accept. This is intentional — it ensures the home campus has actively reviewed the plan and made any needed adjustments before the student walks back in.

**Q: What if a student is SPED and we need to do an MDR?**
Waypoint handles this automatically. When a SPED or 504 student is referred to DAEP, a compliance hold blocks the placement. The hold doesn't lift until the MDR and compliance checklist are complete. You'll see the hold on your dashboard and the Incident Detail page.
